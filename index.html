The AI in Language Learning & Assessment Lab

The artificial intelligence in language learning and assessment lab is a group of researchers and innovators who are interested in developing innovative language learning and assessment applications using cutting edge AI

Objectives


1. To build a community of researchers and inventors in language learning and assessment.
2. To develop AI-based technologies and products for improving language learning and assessment.
3. To investigate societal and ethical issues pertaining to the use of AI powered language learning and assessment technologies.

Research Strands & Current Projects

Visual Complexity in Instructional Videos

Through multiple experiments, this project aims to assess the impact of visual complexity on viewing comprehension and develop a set of design principles for effective use of visuals in instructional videos.

AUVANA

Visual complexity is widely considered to be an important variable underlying visual perception. While videos have become versatile in their use of visual imagery, surprisingly, little research has been devoted to understanding the impact of visual complexity. In this paper, we present Automated Video Analysis (AUVANA) software, an open-source tool for extracting, computing, and visualizing visual complexity in digital videos. Through leveraging more sophisticated computer vision and video processing algorithms, AUVANA automatically extracts and computes 78 video visual complexity indices.

Preprint Code
Arabic Text Readability

Text readability is essential for developing reading literacy and a prerequisite for developing other skills. The aim of the Arabic readability project is to investigate what makes Arabic texts difficult for students in general education (K1 -12) and to develop an open-source web application for automatically assessing text readability in Arabic using a machine learning approach.

Automatic Adjustment of Text Difficulty

The aim of this project is to examine the impact of adjusting the level of reading difficulty on language learners’ understanding of reading text. To this end, we will design and develop a novel automatic text simplification (ATS) web application for automatically adjusting the reading difficulty of texts based on real-time analysis of text, learners’ eye-movements, and facial temperatures.

Arabic Font Design and Complexity

In a series of experiments, the project aims to examine the interplay between Arabic font aesthetics and complexity and their impact on readers’ mode, reading speed and comprehension. The findings will be then used to develop a system that generate new scripts that optimize one’s reading experience.

CrowdyQ: A Decentralized Platform for Item Development at a Scale

Writing exam items by a small, designated committee of exam developers is a common practice in colleges and universities around the world. However, some educational institutions might not have skilful exam developers and proper resources. To address this issue, we propose CrowdyQ, a virtual crowdsourcing platform, to allow naive instructors and professional test developers to create and evaluate question items in a collaborative manner for the purpose of developing high quality exam items.
